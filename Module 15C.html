Factory Testing, Commissioning, and Maintenance Testing

Testing an Electrologixs unit is a critical and systematic process performed throughout its lifecycle to ensure safe and reliable railway signaling operation. Testing verifies that the unit correctly executes the programmed vital interlocking logic and properly interfaces with all connected field equipment.

Factory acceptance testing includes comprehensive checks of the Electrologixs hardware, including the central processing unit, input and output modules, and communication interfaces. Simulation tools are used to test the programmed logic under both normal and fault conditions.

These simulations confirm that the software properly enforces the intended safety rules and responds correctly to different inputs. Successful factory testing is required before the Electrologixs unit is placed into railway service.

On-site testing and commissioning are performed after the unit is installed and connected to real field equipment such as signals, switches, track circuits, and level crossings. This includes checking wiring, communication, route locking, signal aspects, switch positions, track occupancy, and crossing controls.

Manufacturer diagnostic tools are used to monitor system performance and identify any discrepancies. Ongoing maintenance testing is also required to ensure the long-term safety, reliability, and proper operation of Electrologixs systems.
