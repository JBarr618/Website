Module 6B: Why is an AC Double Rail Track Circuit Used?

AC double rail track circuits are primarily used in railway signaling systems for two key reasons:

1. Compatibility with Electrified Railways: In electrified railways, the traction power supplied to trains is typically alternating current (AC). This AC current flows through the rails, returning to the substation via the rails or dedicated return conductors. Introducing DC track circuits in such an environment would lead to interference and potential disruptions. AC double rail track circuits, with their inherent compatibility with AC traction, are designed to function reliably even in the presence of this high-current return flow.

2. Improved Reliability in AC Traction Areas: In addition to compatibility, AC double rail track circuits offer a significant advantage in terms of reliability. The impedance bonds used in AC double rail circuits help to minimize the effects of traction return currents on the track circuit itself. This reduces the risk of false indications or system malfunctions caused by the presence of the traction current.

Overall, AC double rail track circuits are a more suitable and reliable option for train detection in electrified railway environments compared to DC track circuits. They provide a robust and accurate means of monitoring track occupancy and ensuring the safe operation of trains even in the presence of high-voltage traction power.
