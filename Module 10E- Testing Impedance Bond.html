Module 10E: Testing Impedance Bonds

Testing impedance bonds is crucial to ensure they are functioning correctly and providing both a low-impedance path for traction return current and a high impedance to signaling frequencies. Regular testing helps to identify degradation or failures that could impact both the traction and signaling systems. Key tests include:

  • DC Resistance Measurement: Verifies low resistance for traction return; high resistance may indicate faults. Measured with a low-resistance ohmmeter.
  • Inductance Measurement: Ensures proper impedance at relevant frequencies; tested using an LCR meter.
  • Impedance at Operating Frequencies: Confirms low impedance at traction frequency and high impedance at signaling frequency; measured with frequency-capable impedance meters.
  • Current Carrying Capacity: In some cases, bonds are tested under high-current conditions to check durability and thermal limits.
  • Insulation Resistance: Verifies no leakage or shorting between windings and frame using an insulation tester (megger).
  • Visual Inspection: Looks for corrosion, damage, looseness, or signs of overheating at terminals or mounting points.

Testing frequency and documentation are governed by maintenance standards and regulations to ensure long-term safety and reliability.
