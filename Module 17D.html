What Does Operation Lifesaver Do for Crossings?

Operation Lifesaver (OLI) is a non-profit public safety organization dedicated to reducing collisions, injuries, and fatalities at highway-rail grade crossings and along railroad rights-of-way.

Although Operation Lifesaver does not directly install or maintain crossing warning systems, the organization plays an important role in improving railroad crossing safety through education, enforcement, and engineering, commonly called the "3 Es."

Education is one of Operation Lifesaver’s primary focuses. The organization provides public safety presentations, educational programs, videos, brochures, and online resources that teach drivers and pedestrians how to safely approach and cross railroad tracks.

Operation Lifesaver also works with law enforcement agencies to support enforcement of crossing safety laws. This includes discouraging dangerous actions such as driving around lowered gates or ignoring flashing warning lights.

Through engineering support and advocacy, Operation Lifesaver encourages improvements such as better crossing visibility, installation of flashing lights and gates, and safer roadway designs at crossings with increased risk.

The organization also supports programs such as Operation Clear Track, which focuses on railroad crossing safety enforcement and reducing trespassing incidents near railroad property.

Overall, Operation Lifesaver serves as an important safety resource that promotes awareness, encourages safer driver behavior, and supports improvements that reduce accidents at highway-rail grade crossings.
