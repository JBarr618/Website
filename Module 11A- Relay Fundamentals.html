Module 11A: Relay Fundamentals

To understand how relays are used in railway signaling, it's important to grasp some fundamental concepts related to their operation and characteristics:

  • Coil Voltage and Current: Relays require a specific voltage and current to energize their coil. Too little voltage may fail to activate the relay; too much may damage the coil.
  • Contact Configuration:
    • Normally Open (NO): Open when de-energized, closed when energized.
    • Normally Closed (NC): Closed when de-energized, open when energized.
    • Changeover (CO or DPDT): Switches between NO and NC positions.
  • Operating Time: Time delay between energizing the coil and contact actuation, typically in milliseconds.
  • Contact Rating: Specifies the maximum voltage and current the relay contacts can switch safely.
  • Holding Current: The lower current needed to keep the relay energized after activation.
  • Fail-Safe Design: Ensures a safe output state (like "stop") in case of power failure, often using spring or gravity return.

Understanding these characteristics is essential for designing, installing, and maintaining safe and reliable railway signaling circuits using relays.
