Module 8B: Transmitter and Receiver Roles in PSO Circuits

In a Phase Shift Overlay (PSO) track circuit, the transmitter and receiver each play a critical role in ensuring reliable detection of train occupancy. The transmitter generates a continuous low-voltage AC signal at a designated frequency. This signal is fed into the track at one end of the block and travels through the rails toward the receiver at the opposite end.

The receiver is tuned to detect not only the frequency but also the precise phase angle of the received signal. By comparing the phase of the received signal with a reference signal generated locally, the receiver can detect any phase shift caused by changes in track impedance. When a train enters the block, the wheels and axles shunt the rails, altering the impedance and thus shifting the phase of the signal. A defined threshold in phase change indicates track occupancy.

The accurate performance of the transmitter and receiver is essential to the success of a PSO circuit. If the transmitter does not provide a stable frequency, or if the receiver is not accurately calibrated to the expected phase characteristics, false indications may occur. Therefore, both devices must be carefully installed, calibrated, and maintained to ensure proper signaling behavior.
