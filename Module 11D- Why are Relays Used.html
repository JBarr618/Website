Module 11D: Why are Relays Used?

Relays are essential components in electrical and electronic systems, and particularly critical in railway signaling, due to a multitude of compelling reasons:

  1. Remote Control Capabilities: Relays allow low-power signals to operate high-power devices at distant locations — ideal for controlling signals and switches remotely.
  2. Electrical Isolation and Safety: Relays isolate the control circuit from the controlled circuit, protecting people and equipment from electrical hazards across different voltage levels.
  3. Signal Amplification: A small coil current can switch a much larger load current via the contacts, allowing sensors or controllers to operate heavy-duty devices.
  4. Implementation of Logic Functions: Combinations of relays can create electrical logic for control systems (AND, OR, NOT), which was historically vital for interlocking in railway signaling.
  5. Fail-Safe Operation: Relays are designed to revert to a safe state (e.g., displaying "stop") during failures such as power loss or component malfunction.
  6. Interfacing Different Systems: Relays bridge electrical systems that use different voltages or currents, acting as adapters or buffers between incompatible devices.

Because of these capabilities, relays remain foundational in ensuring the safety, reliability, and operational control of modern railway signaling systems.
