Module 13C: Troubleshooting Microlok Units

What is a Microlok Unit?

Microlok refers to a family of vital electronic interlocking systems originally developed by General Railway Signal (GRS). Over time, GRS became part of Alstom in 1998.1 However, the Microlok product line is now associated with Wabtec, following subsequent acquisitions and industry restructuring. Microlok is a widely used EPTC system known for its reliability, flexibility, and application in both mainline and mass transit railway signaling.2

[Image: Microlok Diagnostic Software Screenshot]

A Microlok unit, at its core, is a programmable electronic controller designed for safety-critical railway interlocking applications. It receives inputs from field devices such as track circuits, switches, and signals, processes this information according to the configured interlocking logic, and generates vital outputs to control these field devices, ensuring safe train movements.

Key characteristics of a Microlok unit and the broader Microlok system include:

  • Vital Processing: Microlok units employ microprocessor-based technology with built-in safety features to ensure that outputs are only generated when safe conditions are met. This “vital” processing is crucial for preventing conflicting train movements and ensuring safe signaling.
  • Software-Based Logic: The interlocking logic for a specific railway installation is implemented through software programming.3 This offers significant flexibility for adapting to different track layouts and operational requirements, and for making future modifications.
  • Modular Architecture: Microlok systems typically have a modular design, allowing for scalability and customization based on the complexity of the interlocking.4 Input/output (I/O) modules interface with field equipment, and communication modules allow for connection to other signaling and control systems.
  • Self-Diagnostics and Monitoring: Microlok units often include comprehensive self-monitoring and diagnostic capabilities to detect internal faults and provide status information, aiding in maintenance and troubleshooting.5
  • Communication Interfaces: Microlok systems can communicate with various other systems using different protocols, including those for remote control panels, CBTC interfaces, and data logging. The Microlok II system, for example, can use serial protocols like RS-232 for communication.6
[Image: Technician Checking Wiring at Microlok Cabinet]

The Microlok product line has evolved over time, with versions like Microlok I and the more widely deployed Microlok II. Microlok II is an FRA-compliant system with a proven track record in North American railway signaling. It is designed to handle a variety of applications, including basic interlocking control, speed command logic for automatic train control (ATC), and coded track circuit functions. Hitachi Rail has also developed systems, such as MicroLok II Genesys, building upon the Microlok II platform.

In essence, a Microlok unit is a powerful and reliable electronic brain of a railway interlocking system, replacing traditional relays with software-driven safety logic to control and monitor train movements safely and efficiently.
