Railroad Signals and Train Control

Railroad signals are the language of the railway, conveying essential information to train crews regarding track occupancy, authorized speed, and movement authority. These signals are strategically placed along the tracks and utilize a combination of lights, colors, and sometimes mechanical indicators to communicate these instructions.

The evolution of railroad signals has been a journey driven by the need for increased safety and efficiency. Early methods relied on manual flagmen and time-based train schedules, which were prone to human error and limited the capacity of the railway.

The introduction of electric light signals marked a significant advancement, offering better visibility in various weather conditions and allowing for more complex signaling aspects. Today, modern railroads utilize sophisticated color light signals and computer-based interlocking systems.
