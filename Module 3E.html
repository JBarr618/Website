Direct Current Track Circuits - Testing a DC Track Circuit

Regular and thorough testing of DC track circuits is important to ensure reliable operation and the safe movement of trains. Testing verifies the electrical condition of the circuit and confirms that the track relay responds correctly.

One basic test involves measuring the voltage and current at the relay end of the track circuit under normal unoccupied conditions. These readings should be within the established operating limits so the relay remains properly energized when the track is clear.

Another critical test is the shunt test. This test simulates the presence of a train by placing a low-resistance shunt across the rails at different locations within the track block.

When the shunt is applied, the relay should quickly de-energize and indicate that the track is occupied. This confirms that the circuit can detect a train or shunt condition reliably.

The insulation resistance between the rails and ground should also be checked periodically. Low insulation resistance can cause current leakage, reduce relay current, and create false occupied indications.

The operation of the relay itself must also be tested to confirm that the contacts move properly in both the energized and de-energized states. Test results should be recorded and compared with previous readings to identify trends or problems before they become failures.
