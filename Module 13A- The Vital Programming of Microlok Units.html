Module 13A: The Vital Programming of Microlok Units

The vital programming of Microlok units relies on a proprietary, high-level, safety-oriented programming language and a dedicated Integrated Development Environment (IDE) provided by Wabtec (originally GRS). This specialized software suite is engineered with a paramount focus on safety and reliability, essential for controlling critical railway interlocking functions. The high-level nature of the language allows signaling engineers to define complex interlocking logic using terms and concepts directly related to railway signaling principles, such as routes, signals, switches, and track occupancy.

[Image: Screenshot of Microlok IDE Interface]

The development environment for Microlok includes features specifically designed to aid in the creation of safe and dependable interlocking programs. Simulation and testing tools are crucial components, allowing engineers to thoroughly verify the behavior of the programmed interlocking under various operational scenarios and fault conditions in a safe, offline environment. Wabtec provides extensive documentation and training on the proper use of this software and the principles of writing safe vital logic.

[Image: Microlok Workflow Diagram]

Given the safety-critical nature of railway interlocking systems, the specifics of the Microlok programming language and IDE are generally proprietary to Wabtec. This controlled environment ensures that only trained and authorized personnel can develop and modify the vital logic, and that the tools used have been rigorously tested and validated for safety.
