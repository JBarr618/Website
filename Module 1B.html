Module 1B: Signal System Overview

This module expands on the basics of railroad signals by examining how signals interact with track circuits and interlocking logic. Track circuits are the backbone of occupancy detection and play a critical role in safe train separation.

Signals can be controlled manually from a signal tower or automatically through coded track circuits or a computer-based interlocking (CBI). Understanding these connections provides a deeper view of how trains are routed, held, or cleared to proceed safely.

We'll also look at fail-safe principles and redundancy in signal system design. These are crucial to ensuring that a failure of any single component defaults the system to a safe state, preventing unsafe train movements.
