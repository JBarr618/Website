Testing AC Double Rail Track Circuits

Testing AC double rail track circuits is important to verify proper operation and support safe train movement, especially in electrified territory. Testing confirms the condition of the electrical circuit, receiver, shunt sensitivity, phase relationship, impedance bonds, and insulation resistance.

One important test is measuring the voltage and current at the receiver end while the track is clear. These readings must be within the specified operating range so the receiver can reliably indicate an unoccupied track.

Shunt sensitivity testing is also critical. A low-impedance shunt is applied across the rails at different points in the block to simulate a train. The AC receiver should quickly and reliably de-energize when the shunt is applied.

The phase relationship between the transmitted AC signal and the receiver reference must also be checked. Incorrect phase can cause unreliable detection, weak operation, or incorrect receiver response.

Impedance bonds must be tested to verify that they allow traction return current to pass while helping protect the track circuit signal. This may include measuring impedance at traction frequency and track circuit frequency.

Insulation resistance testing between the rails and ground is also performed to check for leakage paths. All test results should be documented and compared with standards and previous readings to identify trends or potential problems.
