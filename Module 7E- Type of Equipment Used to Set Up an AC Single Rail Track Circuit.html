Module 7E: Type of Equipment Used to Set Up an AC Single Rail Track Circuit

Setting up an AC single rail track circuit involves a specific set of equipment tailored to its unique configuration:

  • Insulated Rail Joints: These non-conductive joints are installed on only one of the two running rails to electrically isolate the track block from adjacent sections.
  • AC Power Source: This device, typically a transformer or oscillator, generates the alternating current signal that is fed into the track circuit. The frequency and voltage output are critical and must match the specifications of the receiver.
  • AC Receiver: This is the detection unit, often an AC-sensitive relay or an electronic phase-selective device, located at the opposite end of the track block from the power source. It is designed to respond to the presence or absence (due to train shunting) of the specific AC signal.
  • Connecting Wires/Cables: These are used to connect the AC power source and the AC receiver to the insulated and continuous rails. The gauge and insulation of these wires must be appropriate for the voltage and current involved.
  • Bonding Straps (Optional): In some installations, particularly around insulated joints or to ensure good electrical continuity of the continuous rail, bonding straps might be used.
  • Test and Calibration Equipment: This includes multimeters (capable of measuring AC voltage and current), oscilloscopes (for analyzing the AC waveform), and specialized shunt testing devices to verify the circuit's operation.
