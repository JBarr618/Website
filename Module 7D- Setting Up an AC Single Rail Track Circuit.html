Module 7D: Setting Up an AC Single Rail Track Circuit

Setting up an AC single rail track circuit involves a specific procedure that accounts for the continuous nature of one rail and the insulation of the other. The first crucial step is to identify and insulate only one of the running rails at the boundaries of the desired track block using insulated rail joints. The other rail remains continuous and electrically connected across the block. The length of the track circuit block is determined by signaling design principles.

Next, the AC power source (typically a transformer) is connected between the insulated rail and the continuous rail at one end of the track block. Polarity is important, and connections must be secure to ensure reliable signal transmission. Similarly, the AC receiver (relay or electronic detection unit) is connected between the same insulated rail and the continuous rail at the opposite end of the track block. The operating frequency and voltage of the power source must be compatible with the receiver.

Unlike double rail circuits, impedance bonds are generally not required at the insulated joints in a basic AC single rail track circuit, as the continuous rail provides a direct path for traction return currents (if applicable). However, in some more complex installations or where there are specific interference concerns, small impedance matching devices might be used. Finally, thorough testing and calibration are essential to ensure the receiver correctly detects the presence of a shunt (simulating a train) between the insulated and continuous rails at various points within the block. Voltage and current levels at the receiver must be within specified limits for reliable operation.
