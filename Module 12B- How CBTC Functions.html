🚦 Module 12B - How CBTC Functions

How Computer Based Train Control Functions

Computer Based Train Control, or CBTC, works by continuously collecting train information, sending that information to control equipment, calculating safe movement limits, and supervising the train as it moves.

Unlike older fixed block signal systems, CBTC uses real-time information about train location, train speed, direction of travel, and safe braking distance. This allows trains to operate closer together while still maintaining a safe separation.

1. Train Localization

Each train determines its location using onboard equipment such as odometers, speed sensors, and fixed reference points along the track. These reference points may include balises, transponders, or other location devices.

2. Communication

The train sends its location, speed, direction, and operating status to the CBTC control system. This communication is usually wireless and occurs continuously or very frequently.

3. Central Processing

The CBTC control system receives information from all trains in the system. It also considers track layout, curves, grades, speed limits, switch positions, interlocking status, and the location of other trains.

Using this information, the system calculates how far each train may safely travel. This safe travel limit is called Movement Authority.

4. Movement Authority

Movement Authority tells the train how far it is allowed to proceed. The system may also send a speed profile, which tells the train the maximum safe speed for different parts of the route.

5. Onboard ATP Supervision

The onboard Automatic Train Protection system, or ATP, monitors the train against the received Movement Authority and speed profile.

If the train exceeds the permitted speed or gets too close to the end of its Movement Authority, ATP automatically applies the brakes to keep the train safe.

6. Continuous Updates

CBTC is a continuous process. As trains move, the system constantly updates train positions, recalculates safe movement limits, and sends new Movement Authorities as needed.

7. Interface with Other Systems

CBTC also works with other railroad systems such as interlockings, switches, route controls, traffic management systems, and station equipment.
