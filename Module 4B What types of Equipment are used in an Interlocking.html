Module 4B: What Types of Equipment are Used in an Interlocking

Interlockings rely on a combination of mechanical, electrical, and electronic components to achieve their vital functions. The core elements include:

  • Track Switches (Points): Movable sections of track that allow trains to change direction or switch between tracks. Typically operated by switch machines, either manually or remotely.
  • Signals: Visual and audible devices that inform train crews about route status, speed limits, and safety procedures, interconnected with interlocking logic.
  • Interlocking Logic: A complex network of circuits and logic gates that coordinate switches and signals, ensuring only one valid route at a time.
  • Switch Machines: Motorized devices used to control the movement of track switches, operated from control towers or signal centers.
  • Signal Machines: Devices (electronic or electro-mechanical) that control lights and indications of signals.
  • Track Circuits: Electrical circuits that detect train presence on a track section and feed information to the interlocking logic.

Modern interlockings also use computers, microprocessors, and digital communications for advanced control, real-time monitoring, and remote diagnostics.
