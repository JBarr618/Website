Module 9C: Setting Up a Type C Track Circuit

Setting up a Type C track circuit is a complex process that requires careful installation and calibration of specialized equipment to ensure the reliable transmission and reception of the two distinct AC frequencies. The key steps generally involve:

  1. Defining the Track Block: The physical boundaries of the track block are defined using insulated rail joints on both rails. This ensures electrical isolation for the signaling currents.
  2. Dual-Frequency Transmitter Installation: A transmitter capable of simultaneously generating the two required AC frequencies is installed. Output levels and frequency stability must be precisely calibrated.
  3. Dual-Frequency Receiver Installation: A receiver separates and analyzes both AC frequencies using filters and detection circuits to monitor amplitude and/or phase changes.
  4. Wiring and Connections: Proper wiring with correct impedance ensures minimal signal distortion and loss across both frequencies.
  5. Tuning and Calibration: The receiver’s filters are tuned to the transmitted frequencies. Baseline signals and thresholds for occupancy and broken rail detection are established under clear track conditions.
  6. Testing and Verification: Testing confirms the system detects train shunts and simulated rail breaks. Immunity to AC traction interference is also evaluated.
