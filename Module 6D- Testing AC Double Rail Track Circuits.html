Module 6D: Testing AC Double Rail Track Circuits

Testing AC double rail track circuits is crucial to ensure their proper operation and the safety of train movements, particularly in electrified territories. The testing procedures focus on verifying the integrity of the electrical circuit, the sensitivity of the receiver, and the effectiveness of the impedance bonds. One essential test involves measuring the voltage and current at the receiver end under normal (unoccupied) conditions. These values must be within the specified operating range to guarantee reliable detection.

Another critical aspect of testing is the shunt sensitivity. This test simulates a train entering the block by applying a low-impedance shunt across the rails at various points. The AC receiver should reliably and quickly de-energize when the shunt is applied, indicating occupancy. The phase relationship between the transmitted AC signal and the receiver's internal reference is also meticulously checked and adjusted. An incorrect phase relationship can lead to unreliable detection.

Furthermore, the effectiveness of the impedance bonds in allowing the passage of low-frequency AC traction return currents without affecting the higher-frequency track circuit signal is rigorously tested. This often involves measuring the impedance across the bonds at both the traction frequency and the track circuit frequency. Finally, regular insulation resistance tests between the rails and the ground are performed to ensure minimal leakage, which could affect the track circuit's performance. All test results should be carefully documented and compared with established standards and historical data to identify any potential issues.
