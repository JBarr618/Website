Module 8D: Equipment Used in Phase Shift Overlay Track Circuits

Setting up a Phase Shift Overlay (PSO) track circuit requires specialized equipment designed for generating, transmitting, and detecting subtle changes in the phase of an AC signal:

  • PSO Transmitter: This unit generates a continuous AC signal at a specific, stable frequency and power level. It often includes circuitry for precise phase control and output impedance matching.
  • PSO Receiver: A sophisticated electronic device designed to detect minute changes in the phase of the received AC signal relative to a local reference. It typically incorporates phase comparators, filters, and processing circuitry to reliably identify train occupancy and potentially broken rails.
  • Tuned Circuits/Impedance Matching Units: Passive components (inductors and capacitors) placed at the boundaries of the track block. They help define the electrical length of the track circuit and minimize signal reflections, ensuring a consistent phase relationship under clear track conditions.
  • Transmission Lines/Cables: High-quality cables with controlled impedance characteristics are used to connect the transmitter and receiver to the rails, ensuring minimal signal distortion and loss.
  • Phase Meter/Analyzer: Specialized test equipment for calibrating and verifying the phase relationships within the PSO system. It allows technicians to precisely measure the phase difference between transmitted and received signals under different track conditions.
  • Shunt Application Device: A calibrated shunt used during testing to simulate the presence of a train and verify the receiver’s detection threshold.
