Module 10D: Types of Impedance Bonds Used

Various types of impedance bonds are designed to meet the specific requirements of different railway electrification and signaling systems. The main distinctions lie in their construction, current carrying capacity, and suitability for different frequencies and track circuit types. Here are some common types:

  • Single Impedance Bonds: Basic type with a single winding, used to pass traction current while blocking signaling frequencies.
  • Center-Tapped Impedance Bonds: Include a center tap for balanced traction return and are often used in double-rail circuits.
  • Double Impedance Bonds: Two single bonds in series to handle higher current or impedance needs.
  • Tuned Impedance Bonds: Include capacitive components tuned for specific signaling frequencies to enhance isolation.
  • Wee-Zee Bonds: Used in DC traction with AC track circuits to shunt DC return and block AC signals.
  • Specialty Bonds: Designed for specific systems like AFTC or impulse track circuits with tailored frequency characteristics.

The right type of bond depends on traction current type, signaling frequency, and system requirements.
