History of the Track Circuit

How it is the Backbone since 1800’s

Doctor (really) a Doctor invented the Track Circuit. Shows anyone can invented something. Yes, William Robinson graduated college in New York in 1867. The system was developed on the open circuit concept. There were circuit instruments near the tracks that the wheels of the train would activate. The circuit would close through the RELAY whose magnet would pull an armature closed to keep the circuit closed until the wheels were not present. Thus, inventing a SIGNAL SYSTEM. Dr. Robinson realized; liked anyone who likes to tinker found that there are defects in the open circuit design. So, he invented another way to make the track circuit safe the closed-circuit concept. What is amazing is that he wanted to patent the track circuit again as a closed-circuit concept which is the backbone of every track circuit TODAY. He headed to the railroad conference again to display his new closed-circuit concept. He set up rails underwater, a gong connected to the wires and wires to the back of the TRACK RELAY. The train wheels on the rails under the water causing a short circuit and ringing the gong. .

What Will Happen When A Track Circuit fails

A Signal Location(Wayside) will display all red aspects in all directions of travel. Train headlights will soon be backing up down the right of way at the control point or interlocking. Your railroad radio keying up and cell phone ringing. Dispatchers (block operators) wanting more information as to when it will be fixed, only wanting to move the trains (Consist) safely through affected area. Maybe it is a Highway-Rail Grade Crossing Warning System Location. The gates will be activated the lights are flashing and the bells are going off loud as heck. Traffic is backed up a mile or two with cars honking their horns and people are driving around the gates. Leaving you no way to get into fix the trouble. Troubleshooting a track circuit is an art form. Now a days you can use electronic meters or use your senses. My go to method is to use my roller decks to phone a friend. While on the way to the location to fix the problem, it is awesome to talk to someone you know in the industry to keep you clam and get your mind thinking of the possible causes of the trouble.
