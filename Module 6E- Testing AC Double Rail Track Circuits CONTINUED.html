Module 6E: Testing AC Double Rail Track Circuits (Continued)

A crucial aspect of AC double rail track circuit testing, especially in electrified areas, is verifying the integrity and balance of the impedance bonds. An imbalance in the impedance of the two bonds at the ends of a track circuit can lead to unwanted AC traction currents flowing through the receiver, potentially causing false "clear" indications. Specialized testing equipment is used to measure the impedance of each bond and ensure they are within acceptable tolerance levels.

Furthermore, broken rail detection capabilities are often tested in AC track circuits. A break in one of the rails should disrupt the flow of the AC signal to the receiver, causing it to de-energize and indicate an occupied (and potentially broken) track. This functionality is vital for safety. The testing involves simulating a rail break and observing the receiver's response.

Finally, regular maintenance testing is essential. This includes visual inspections of all components (insulated joints, impedance bonds, wiring), checking for any signs of damage or corrosion, and repeating the electrical tests to ensure the track circuit continues to operate within its specified parameters over time. Keeping detailed records of all tests and maintenance activities is crucial for identifying trends and ensuring the long-term reliability of the system.
