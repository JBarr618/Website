Module 11H: Troubleshooting Relays

When a relay malfunctions in a railway signaling circuit, it can lead to issues such as signal failures or interlocking errors. A structured approach is essential for effective diagnosis and resolution. Common problems include:

  1. Relay Not Energizing: Could be due to incorrect voltage, open coil, or wiring faults.
  2. Contacts Not Switching: May result from mechanical obstruction, damaged contacts, or broken linkage.
  3. Intermittent Operation: Often caused by loose connections, unstable voltage, environmental factors, or vibration.
  4. Contacts Sticking/Welding: Usually due to overloaded contacts; requires proper rating and protection like snubbers.
  5. Excessive Noise or Vibration: Could point to low coil voltage, faulty AC supply, or loose components.

Always disconnect power before inspecting or adjusting relays, and refer to circuit diagrams and manufacturer specifications to guide the process safely.
