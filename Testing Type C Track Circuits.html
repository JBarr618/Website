Module 9E: Testing Type C Track Circuits

Testing AC Type C track circuits involves a rigorous process to verify their ability to accurately detect trains and broken rails, while also ensuring their robustness against interference and other environmental factors. Here are the key steps:

  1. Baseline Phase Measurements: Establish phase references under clear track conditions for both frequencies used—one for train detection, the other for broken rail detection.
  2. Shunt Testing: Apply calibrated shunts at multiple locations to simulate train presence and verify receiver response on both frequencies without triggering false broken rail indications.
  3. Broken Rail Simulation: Introduce simulated breaks and verify the receiver accurately identifies changes in the broken rail detection frequency.
  4. Frequency Stability Checks: Monitor transmitter frequency output regularly. Frequency drift can compromise detection accuracy.
  5. Signal Level Monitoring: Measure amplitude of both transmitted and received signals to detect loss or circuit issues.
  6. Environmental Testing: Evaluate performance under temperature, moisture, and electromagnetic challenges.
  7. Interference Testing: Inject simulated AC traction interference to confirm the circuit’s immunity to noise.
