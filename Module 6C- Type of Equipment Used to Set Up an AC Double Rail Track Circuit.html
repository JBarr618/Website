Module 6C: Type of Equipment Used to Set Up an AC Double Rail Track Circuit

Setting up an AC double rail track circuit requires several key pieces of equipment, each serving a specific purpose:

  • Insulated Rail Joints: These are non-conductive joints that electrically isolate adjacent track sections, defining the boundaries of the track block. They are typically made of materials like porcelain or fiber glass.
  • Impedance Bonds: These are inductive devices connected across the rails at the insulated joints. They allow the traction return current to flow while minimizing interference with the AC track circuit signal.
  • AC Power Source: This is usually a transformer that supplies the alternating current to the track circuit. The voltage and frequency of the source must be carefully matched to the receiver's specifications.
  • AC Receiver: This device, typically a relay or a more sophisticated electronic unit, detects the presence or absence of a train within the track block based on the AC signal strength.
  • Track Circuit Feeder Cables: These cables carry the AC power from the source to the track rails and the receiver.
  • Bonding Wires: These are used to connect the rails and other components together, ensuring a continuous electrical path for the track circuit.

In addition to these basic components, other specialized equipment may be used depending on the specific requirements of the track circuit installation, such as surge protectors, lightning arrestors, and calibration devices.
