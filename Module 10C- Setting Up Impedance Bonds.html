Module 10C: Setting Up Impedance Bonds

Setting up impedance bonds requires careful installation and connection to ensure they function correctly for both traction return current and signaling isolation. The general steps involved are:

  1. Location Selection: Installed near insulated rail joints that define AC track circuit boundaries, based on signaling design needs.
  2. Mounting: Securely mounted to the track structure to withstand vibration and maintain stable electrical connections.
  3. Rail Connection: Heavy-duty cables or straps connect bond terminals to the rails. Good electrical contact is essential for handling traction current.
  4. Bonding Configuration: Some systems require center-tapped connections or specific wiring depending on circuit design.
  5. Testing and Verification: Measurements are taken for impedance at traction and signaling frequencies, and continuity checks confirm correct operation.

Proper installation and testing are critical for safe and reliable performance. Errors can cause both traction and signaling issues, creating operational hazards.
