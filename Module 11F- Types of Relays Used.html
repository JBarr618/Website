Module 11F: Types of Relays Used

Railway signaling systems utilize a variety of relay types, each suited for specific applications based on their characteristics, reliability, and safety features. Here are some common types:

  • Electromechanical Relays (EMRs): Traditional relays with mechanical movement. Subtypes include:
    • Armature Relays: Use a hinged armature to move contacts; common in general switching.
    • Reed Relays: Sealed in glass tubes; fast and durable but sensitive to shock.
    • Stepping Relays: Mechanically advance with each pulse; used for memory/sequencing.
  • Latching Relays: Maintain contact position after coil is de-energized; useful for state retention.
  • Solid-State Relays (SSRs): Use semiconductor components for fast, silent switching; no moving parts.
  • Safety Relays: Have force-guided contacts; ensure all contacts move together in a fault for maximum safety.
  • Time Delay Relays: Introduce intentional switching delay for sequencing or timing operations.

Relay choice depends on function, reliability, environmental conditions, and safety level requirements. For safety-critical circuits, safety relays or fail-safe EMRs are usually required.
