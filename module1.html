Understanding Basic Railroad Signal Systems

Railroad signaling systems are designed to safely control train movement, protect workers, prevent collisions, and manage traffic flow across the railroad.

Signal systems use track circuits, relays, switches, signals, and control logic to determine train occupancy and authorize train movement.

Modern railroad systems may include Centralized Traffic Control (CTC), Positive Train Control (PTC), and Communication Based Train Control (CBTC).
