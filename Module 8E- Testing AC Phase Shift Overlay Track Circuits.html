Module 8E: Testing AC Phase Shift Overlay Track Circuits

Testing AC Phase Shift Overlay (PSO) track circuits requires specialized procedures and equipment to verify their ability to accurately detect train occupancy and broken rails through the analysis of signal phase. Key aspects of PSO testing include:

  1. Baseline Phase Measurement: Initially, the phase relationship between the transmitted and received signals is precisely measured and recorded under clear track conditions. This baseline phase is the reference point against which changes due to train occupancy or rail breaks will be compared.
  2. Shunt Testing: A calibrated shunt is applied at various locations within the track block to simulate the presence of a train. The phase shift at the receiver is carefully measured for each shunt location. The system must demonstrate a consistent and detectable phase shift that exceeds the predetermined occupancy threshold.
  3. Broken Rail Simulation: To verify the broken rail detection capability, a simulated break (an open circuit) is introduced in one or both rails at different points within the block. The resulting phase shift at the receiver is observed. A significant phase change should occur, indicating a fault condition. The sensitivity of the system to different types and locations of breaks is evaluated.
  4. Frequency Stability Check: The stability of the transmitter's output frequency is crucial for reliable phase detection. The frequency is monitored over time to ensure it remains within the specified tolerances.
  5. Signal Level Measurement: While phase is the primary detection parameter, the amplitude of the received signal is also monitored to ensure it remains within an acceptable range. Significant signal loss could indicate other problems within the track circuit.
  6. Environmental Condition Testing: The performance of the PSO system under various environmental conditions (e.g., temperature changes, moisture) may also be evaluated to ensure reliable operation in real-world scenarios.
