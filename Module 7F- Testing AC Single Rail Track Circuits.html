Module 7F: Testing AC Single Rail Track Circuits

Testing AC single rail track circuits is essential to ensure their reliable operation and the accurate detection of trains. The testing procedures focus on verifying the electrical integrity between the insulated and continuous rails within the defined block and the sensitivity of the AC receiver.

One fundamental test involves measuring the voltage and current at the receiver end under normal (unoccupied) conditions. These readings must be within the specified operating parameters for the receiver to function correctly.

A crucial test for single rail circuits is the shunt test, where a low-impedance shunt is applied between the insulated rail and the continuous rail at various points within the track block. The AC receiver should reliably and quickly de-energize when the shunt is applied, indicating occupancy. The shunt sensitivity of the circuit needs to be verified to ensure it can detect a train even with some ballast leakage.

Because only one rail is insulated, specific attention is paid to the insulation resistance between the insulated rail and the ground (ballast), as well as the insulation between the insulated rail and the continuous rail outside of the defined block (across the insulated joints). Low insulation resistance can lead to signal leakage and potential false indications. Additionally, the frequency of the AC signal generated by the power source should be checked to ensure it matches the operating frequency of the receiver. Any significant deviation can affect the receiver's sensitivity.
