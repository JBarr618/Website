Relay Inspection and Testing

Testing relays is important because relays provide switching, indication, logic, and safety functions in railway signaling systems.

Regular relay testing helps identify damage, wear, weak coils, bad contacts, loose terminals, and other problems before they affect signal system operation.

A visual inspection should be performed before electrical testing. The technician should look for physical damage, corrosion, cracks, burn marks, loose hardware, damaged terminals, or signs of overheating.

Continuity testing is used to verify that the relay coil and contacts have proper electrical paths. This can help identify open circuits, abnormal resistance, or failed contacts before the relay is energized.

Coil resistance testing is performed by measuring resistance across the relay coil terminals. The reading should be compared to the relay specifications or known good values.

Coil voltage and current testing verifies that the relay operates correctly when the proper rated power is applied. The relay should pick up, move its contacts, and drop away correctly when power is removed.

Contact resistance testing checks the condition of the relay contacts. High contact resistance can cause voltage drop, heat, weak circuit operation, or failure of the controlled circuit.

Operating time testing measures how long it takes for the relay to pick up or drop away. Timing is important in circuits where sequencing, locking, or safety logic depends on relay response.

Functional testing checks the relay while it is working in the actual circuit. This verifies real-world operation, load handling, circuit interaction, and system behavior.

Relay test results should be documented so future readings can be compared. Good records help identify relay degradation before a failure occurs.
