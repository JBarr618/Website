Module 11G: Testing Relays

Testing relays is crucial to ensure they are functioning correctly and providing reliable switching in railway signaling systems. Regular testing helps to identify potential failures or degradation that could disrupt the signaling system and compromise safety.

  1. Visual Inspection: Check for physical damage, corrosion, cracks, burn marks, or loose terminals.
  2. Continuity Test: Use a multimeter to verify proper coil and contact paths when the relay is de-energized.
  3. Coil Resistance Test: Measure resistance across coil terminals and compare to manufacturer specs.
  4. Coil Voltage and Current Test: Apply rated power to the coil and confirm actuation and contact movement.
  5. Contact Resistance Test: Measure closed and open contact resistance during coil energization.
  6. Operating Time Test: Measure the delay between coil energization/de-energization and contact switching.
  7. Leakage Current Test: Test insulation by applying high voltage and checking for leakage current.
  8. Functional Tests in the Circuit: Apply real-world control signals and observe full system behavior.
  9. Periodic Maintenance Testing: Incorporate regular testing intervals to catch early faults before failure.
