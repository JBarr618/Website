Module 10F: Troubleshooting Impedance Bonds

Troubleshooting impedance bond issues often requires understanding how a failure in the bond can affect both the traction power system and the signaling system. Common problems and troubleshooting steps include:

  1. High DC Resistance: Poor traction return current flow, potential voltage drops, or interference with DC track circuits. Check for loose/corroded connections or internal damage.
  2. Incorrect Inductance: May cause AC track circuits to give false clear or occupied indications. Measure inductance; replace the bond if out of spec.
  3. Low Impedance at Signaling Frequencies: Can cause signal leakage between circuits. Measure impedance and replace if too low.
  4. Overheating: May indicate excessive traction draw or internal resistance. Check current rating and inspect for internal faults.
  5. Physical Damage or Corrosion: Cracked casings, insulators, or terminal corrosion require cleaning or full replacement.

Always consult railway maintenance manuals and follow safety procedures when troubleshooting energized equipment.
