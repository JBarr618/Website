Module 13B: Testing Microlok Units

Testing Microlok units is a critical process throughout their lifecycle, from initial factory acceptance to ongoing maintenance in the field. The primary goal of testing is to ensure the vital safety functions operate correctly and reliably, preventing unsafe conditions in railway signaling. This involves verifying the hardware integrity, the programmed interlocking logic, and the proper interface with field equipment. Comprehensive testing ensures that the Microlok unit will perform its safety-critical role as intended.

[Image: Microlok Diagnostic Testing]

Factory testing includes functional checks of the processing unit, input/output modules, and communication interfaces. Simulations are used to test the interlocking logic under normal and fault conditions. Field testing involves verifying physical connections and logic functions with trackside equipment, ensuring safe operation of signals, switches, and routes. Diagnostic tools help monitor system behavior during these steps.

[Image: Commissioning a Microlok Unit]

Ongoing maintenance testing ensures reliability over time. It includes routine checks, diagnostic reviews, and corrective actions as needed. Microlok units must consistently meet safety standards throughout their operational life, requiring trained personnel, detailed documentation, and specialized software to support continuous testing and validation.
